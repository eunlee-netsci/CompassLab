A comprehensive evaluation of entropy-based directionality estimation method

D Kim, E Lee, J Kang
Journal of the Korean Physical Society, 1-12, 2023/8/23

Abstract

Understanding the directionality of interactions in complex networks is crucial for deciphering causal relationships in various systems, from neural circuits to financial markets. Entropy-based methods have emerged as promising approaches for estimating directionality in networks where the underlying causal structure is unknown. However, a comprehensive evaluation of these methods across different network types and conditions has been lacking.

In this study, we conduct a systematic evaluation of entropy-based directionality estimation methods across a range of synthetic and empirical networks with known ground truth. We assess the performance of these methods under varying conditions, including different network sizes, connection densities, noise levels, and temporal resolutions. Our analysis focuses particularly on information-theoretic measures such as transfer entropy, conditional mutual information, and directed information.

Our results reveal that the performance of entropy-based methods varies significantly depending on network characteristics and data properties. We identify specific conditions under which these methods excel or fail, providing practical guidelines for their application. Additionally, we propose modifications to existing methods that improve their robustness in challenging scenarios, such as networks with indirect connections or mixed timescales.

This comprehensive evaluation contributes to a better understanding of the strengths and limitations of entropy-based directionality estimation in complex networks. Our findings and proposed improvements have implications for applications in neuroscience, economics, and other fields where inferring causal relationships from observational data is essential.
