Modeling the dynamics of dissent

E Lee, P Holme, SH Lee
Physica A: Statistical Mechanics and its Applications 486, 262-272, 2017/11/15

Abstract

The act of dissent is central to healthy democracies, scientific progress, and social movements. Despite its importance, the dynamics of how dissent develops and evolves within social systems remains poorly understood. In this paper, we present a model for the dynamics of dissent in social networks with hierarchical structures.

Our model incorporates key features of real-world dissent: the influence of social hierarchy, the role of social reinforcement, and the costs associated with expressing dissenting opinions. We investigate how these factors interact to either facilitate or suppress the spread of dissenting views through a population.

Through numerical simulations, we identify critical thresholds for the emergence of widespread dissent and characterize different regimes of dissent dynamics. Our findings reveal that hierarchical network structures can both inhibit and, under certain conditions, amplify dissenting opinions. We also show how the interplay between individual courage to dissent and social reinforcement mechanisms determines whether dissent remains isolated or spreads throughout the network.

This work provides insights into the conditions under which dissent can overcome social pressure and hierarchical influence, with implications for understanding social movements, scientific revolutions, and political change.
